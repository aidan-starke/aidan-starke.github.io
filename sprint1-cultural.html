My Core Personal Values

1 July 2020

Q1: Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

 

A1: Playing basketball with friends; we were messing around dunking and broke the hoop at a local primary school. I had to argue with my friends and drag them along with me to the office and explain to the lady there what had happened.
Thanks to our honesty and willingness to admit to our mistake, we had no problems with the school and were allowed to continue to use the facilities!

 

Q2: Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

 

A2: My family comes from all over the world - from Fiji to Shetland Island to Europe. I believe that this diversity in my lineage has lead me to be an all round accepting person. I strongly feel that it is important to judge people on their character rather than appearance.

Q3: Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development.

 

A3: I am worried that my confidence may lead me astray sometimes. However, I am never worried to make a mistake, and love having my mistakes pointed out (preferably in a nice way) in order for me to learn more.

 

Q4: Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

 

A4: My willingness to dive in and try, regardless of whether or not I have the necessary knowledge can both work out good or bad. Usually it identifies quickly for me exactly where it is that I need to improve.

 

Q5: Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

 

A5: My latest experience working with others was running a stocktake for my branch of Turner's Cars. It was an incredibly frustrating process as Turner's had just put us through a system migration, and not provided any proper training to myself or to other staff doing stocktake for their branch. I had to adapt quickly, learn the new software, and talk multiple yard managers through the processes they needed to learn and start doing on a day to day basis. Throughout this process, I discovered that the system we had been migrated to did not provide the information our company needed for our cars. That is why I have dived back into the programming world; I have an understanding of what their program needs, and will soon have an understanding of how to make that happen.
