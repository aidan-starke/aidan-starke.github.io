EQ

10 July 2020
What is EQ and why is it important?
EQ stands for Emotional Quotient, though it is more commonly referred to as Emotional Intelligence. Emotions are an intangible aspect of our lives and because of this it can be really tricky to understand them. Emotional Intelligence is the ability to discriminate between your feelings, and use the information they give you as a guide in your thinking and actions.

 

In the tech industry, it is very common to work with many different people. Strong EQ is a key element in anybody who wants to be a leader - it is one of the defining factors that "distinguishes outstanding leaders from those judged as average." (Kemper 1999)
How do we cultivate EQ?
First, we need to understand that EQ is a skill, and just like any other skill, requires practice and maintenance. Practice, combined with a concept called neuroplasticity (the ability of our brain to change based on what we are thinking, doing, and paying attention to), allows anyone to improve these skills.
Training Methods
  1. Attention Training
    2. Also known as mindfulness, attention training is practiced by holding your attention on one thing. Mindfulness is the practice of watching the breath, and also watching your 'self', i.e. watching your attention, and being aware when it slips away from the breath.

     

  2. Conscious Listening
    3. Similarly to mindfulness, this practice is about paying attention. The focus of your attention in conscious listening, is on what another person is saying. It is a great exercise to listen and loop what someone is saying - consciously listen without interrupting, and once the person is done speaking, repeat back to them what you heard them say. The idea is not to make any comments or input on what was heard - simply repeat it back. Repeat this process until the speaker is happy with the listeners understanding of what was said.

     

  3. Journaling
    4. This is about getting accustomed to noticing cognitions and emotions, and being able to write about them. At first it may be difficult, but the goal is to be able to write non-stop for 20 minutes a day. There are many benefits to journaling; not only will it help you understand your self and your emotions, but it deregulates stress and frees the creative side of your brain up!
